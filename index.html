My Story

Vedanti

Dance has always been a passion of mine. From a young age, I was captivated by music's rhythm, which led my mother to introduce me to Bharatnatyam at six. Inspired by my grandmother's devotion to the art, I vividly remember my first class, where I experienced the magical combination of mridangam beats and Carnatic music. Under my first guru, Mrs. Anu Shivram, I learned the importance of discipline and dedication, mastering basic steps, mudras, and expressions through rigorous practice.

As I grew older, my love for Bharatnatyam deepened. I enjoyed performing and portraying mythological characters like Lord Krishna and Lord Shiva. Moving to the UK, I had the honor of studying under Mrs. Meena Raj, who enriched my understanding of dance's history and its cultural significance.

The journey has been both challenging and rewarding, allowing me to grow not just as a dancer but as an individual. Bharatnatyam has taught me the value of perseverance, the joy of storytelling, and the importance of cultural heritage. Each performance is an opportunity to connect with my roots and share the beauty of this classical dance with a wider audience.

Prayer

Pushpam means flowers and Anjali is to offer, Pushpanjali is an invocatory dance where the dancers offer flowers to the Almighty and seek blessings. They then offer their respects to their guru and the audience.

Pushpanjali, Shlokam and Alarippu

Pushpanjali will be followed by a Shlokam in praise of Lord Ganesha and Alarippu set to Misra Chappu Talam (7 beats). Pushpanjali in Ragam Gambheera Nattai and Adhi talam.

Jathiswaram

Jathiswaram is a nritta (pure dance) presentation without any abhinaya (emotions). This dance piece exhibits the execution of a Jathi (rhythmic pattern danced to narrated syllables), intricate footwork (adavus) and hand gestures (mudras) fused to beautiful sequences (korvais) according to the nature of the sequence of swara passages (musical notes) in a particular format.

Jathiswaram: Ragatalamalika
